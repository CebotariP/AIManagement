Possible Legal Approaches

Gathered from McKinsey

Regulations

Government-defined, organization-enforced

Given the ever-changing security, privacy, and ethical risks related to Artificial Intelligence, governments should consider establishing a systematic approach to actively watch for and potentially even penalize organizations that do not effectively identify and manage the risks associated with AI.

Governments can establish AI R&D agencies to work closely with businesses in order to best create a system that is effective and can benefit the economy and society.

Education

Encouraging the public's understanding of AI

Launching free online courses designed to introduce the basics of Artificial Intelligence, much like Finland's government did, can help educate the general public on AI, how they can safely use and navigate it, as well as how they can safely implement it into their lives.

Taking it a step further, governments can follow in Singapore's footsteps and create customizable training courses for certain parts and actors of society.

Research and Innovation

Investing in R&D Brings Greater understanding

Creating agencies that specialize in researching AI and investing a significant amount of money towards building similar institutions can increase our understanding of how to best benefit from AI. Funding researchers and specialists, as well as incentivizing them with grants, could help the government gain more understanding of AI. The government pairing with private R&D institutions can benefit both parties, as well.

Funding

Investment with a large return

All of these suggestions couldn't happen without significant funding - both for the researchers and specialists themselves and for the research and innovation happening. Funds and investment, both from the government and private stakeholds/shareholders could make the difference between efficient and unefficient systems.

Infrastructure

Building a strong foundation

Governments could utilize AI and work hand-in-hand with AI R&D institutions in order to best navigate the physical infrastructure of a town, city, county, state, region, or country. AI could help create better systems regarding vehicles, traffic schemes, road infrastructure, and city planning.

Cooperation

Building Strong relations

Governments cooperating not only locally or regionally, but also internationally, could help navigate and push forward the innovation of Artificial Intelligence. By working closely with trusted local governments and possible international allies, AI can be more efficiently used in order to benefit as many individuals, groups, communities, and societies as possible.
